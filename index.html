HPC in the 20’s - Flexible Performance in the Cloud


To be held as a full-day workshop at ISC 2022

June 2, 2022

　

The continuous increase in complexity and scale of high-end systems is forcing computational scientists to face system characteristics that can significantly impact performance and scalability of applications. HPC users need a flexible and resilient infrastructure that can adapt to their workload needs, rather than having to constantly adapt their applications to new systems. Such infrastructure is available in public clouds today, allowing users to run their applications on the latest hardware generation, scaling their clusters to their workload needs, while paying only for the correct hardware, at the correct time, at the correct scale. However, HPC has been late moving workloads to the cloud, mainly because the initial cloud performance was not great due to network performance and virtualization of the OS, which caused a significant amount of jitter, severely impacting HPC applications. Modern public clouds have addressed these issues. This workshop will focus on the state of the art of cloud computing for HPC. It will cover the cloud software and hardware ecosystem along with user’s experiences, with highlights, success stories, as well as existing gaps. In addition, the Keynote, Dr. Dhabaleswar Panda will talk about designing high-performance communication runtime for HPC and AI workloads in the Cloud.
